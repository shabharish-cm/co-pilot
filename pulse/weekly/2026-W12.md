## Theme 1: Non-Desk Workforce Accessibility

**Prospect:** Cascade Eye & Skin (Amber Gilroy, HR | 300 employees, 7 clinical locations)

Cascade's employees are clinical staff with limited desktop time. Amber explicitly cited this as a core constraint: employees cannot step away during a busy clinical day to take a survey via email on a computer. The platform's multi-channel delivery — email, SMS, QR codes, kiosks, Microsoft Teams — was the primary feature that resonated most strongly in the demo. The ability to send a QR-only open-link survey that any employee can scan without being individually targeted was called out as particularly useful.

**Signal:** Non-desk workforce accessibility (especially clinical, retail, and field workers) is emerging as a recurring ICP differentiator. This mirrors pain points seen in earlier hospitality (El Cortez) and manufacturing calls. The QR + kiosk distribution path is CultureMonkey's strongest differentiator here and should be foregrounded in healthcare-vertical sales decks.

---

## Theme 2: Fresh-Start Engagement Programmes

Cascade has not conducted formal engagement surveys in five years. This is a greenfield implementation with no legacy data, no existing survey culture, and no established cadence. Amber's stated goal was structured feedback "by department and location" — understanding what employees like, dislike, and want improved.

The prospect was unfamiliar with lifecycle surveys until Sunil introduced the concept mid-demo; once explained (ADP-integrated, automated at 15/60/90 days post-hire, linked to exit survey pattern analysis), Amber showed genuine interest. This dynamic — a first-time buyer who needs the value of lifecycle listening explained — is likely common in the SMB/mid-market healthcare segment.

**Signal:** First-time engagement buyers need education on lifecycle surveys as part of the sales motion. Lifecycle value is not assumed. Pre-demo email assets or discovery call scripts that introduce lifecycle value early could increase pipeline conversion for this buyer profile.

---

## Theme 3: Pricing Acceptance & Multi-Year Lock-In

At $8,500/year for 300 employees with no setup fee, Amber confirmed the price "fits within vendor evaluation criteria and budget expectations." She also responded positively to the three-year price lock — indicating that cost stability is a purchase criterion for this type of buyer (likely mid-market, budget-managed HR team).

Cascade plans to evaluate approximately four vendors total (two done at time of call). The deal is live and competitive, not won.

**Signal:** The $8,500/300-employee price point is landing well in the clinical/SMB healthcare segment. The three-year lock-in is a meaningful buying signal for budget-constrained HR buyers. Consider surfacing this early in proposals as a financial risk-reduction argument.

---

## Theme 4: Ask Cooper (AI Chatbot) as a Demo Asset

Sunil demonstrated Ask Cooper during the analytics section, framing it as an on-demand insight tool for managers and HR business partners ("which demographics show the lowest engagement?", "what best practices from high performers can we replicate?"). Amber did not raise questions about it, but it was positioned as a distinct differentiator versus "just a dashboard."

**Signal:** Ask Cooper appears to be gaining traction as a demo differentiator — particularly for buyers who are resource-constrained (small HR teams that don't have time to build reports manually). No pushback or confusion observed. Its value framing ("ChatGPT-like, connected to your platform data") is landing cleanly.
