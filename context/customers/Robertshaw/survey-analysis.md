Robertshaw Employee Engagement Survey Analysis and Briefing

Executive Summary

The 2023-2025 employee engagement initiatives at Robertshaw, conducted via the CultureMonkey platform, reveal a workforce with high participation levels and generally positive engagement sentiment. With a participation rate of approximately 89.8%, the data reflects a robust cross-section of the global organization, which includes a significant population of blue-collar workers.

Key Takeaways:

* Engagement Distribution: 67.4% of employees are "Highly Engaged," while 11.1% are "Actively Disengaged." The overall engagement score stands at 7.5.
* e-NPS Performance: The Employee Net Promoter Score (e-NPS) has fluctuated between 9.21 and 16.72. While scores above zero are considered acceptable, these figures sit within or near the "good" bracket (10–30) by general industry standards.
* Core Philosophy: Engagement is driven by the RESPECT™ model, which posits that employee engagement is directly proportional to the level of respect present in the workplace.
* Operational Context: The transition to a digital platform represents a significant shift from previous manual processes. High adoption rates among managers and a focus on year-over-year score improvement indicate a growing culture of data-driven feedback.

The RESPECT™ Model Framework

The engagement strategy is anchored in Dr. Paul Marciano’s RESPECT™ model, which identifies seven key indicators that influence engagement levels.

The Seven Indicators

1. Recognition: Consistently acknowledging employee contributions.
2. Empowerment: Providing tools, resources, and support for personal development.
3. Supportive Feedback: Delivering timely, constructive coaching.
4. Partnering: Actively promoting teamwork and collaboration.
5. Expectations: Defining clear directions for individuals and teams to ensure accountability.
6. Consideration: Showing care in decision-making and following up on concerns.
7. Trust: Granting autonomy and freedom to make decisions.

17 Drivers of Engagement

These indicators are supported by 17 specific drivers, including Rewards, Growth & Development, Communication, Work-Life Balance, Wellness, and Meaningful Work.

Robertshaw Global Employee Engagement: 2025 Pulse & Insights Analysis Report

1. Executive Survey Framework and Methodology

In the contemporary industrial sector, the ability to maintain organizational agility is predicated on the robustness of internal feedback loops. For Robertshaw, measuring organizational health is a strategic imperative rather than a perfunctory HR task. This 2025 engagement survey serves as the critical baseline for our cultural roadmap, providing the empirical foundation required to transition from anecdotal sentiment to data-driven organizational development.

Methodological Foundation

The survey architecture utilizes the RESPECT™ Model, a framework developed by Dr. Paul Marciano and deployed via the CultureMonkey platform. The core thesis of this methodology is that Employee engagement ∝ Respect. By measuring seven distinct drivers, we can quantify the psychological contract between the employee and the firm:

* Recognition: The consistency of acknowledgment for individual and team contributions.
* Empowerment: The provision of tools, resources, and authority necessary for professional efficacy.
* Supportive Feedback: The delivery of timely, constructive coaching and performance guidance.
* Partnering: The promotion of collaborative behaviors and a "one team" philosophy.
* Expectations: The clarity of direction and accountability standards.
* Consideration: The demonstration of genuine care in decision-making and following up on employee concerns.
* Trust: The granting of autonomy and the freedom for employees to execute their roles.

Survey Logistics & Reach

The 2025 pulse survey achieved a statistically significant participation rate, ensuring the following insights are representative of the global collective.

Metric	Results
Employees Invited	4,755
Surveys Completed	4,270
Participation Rate	89.8%

Communication & Access Strategy

A primary challenge for Robertshaw is reaching the "major trunk" of the workforce—the blue-collar segment—which historically lacked an "online-based" survey culture. To bridge this gap, we deployed a hybrid access strategy utilizing direct Email and localized QR codes. To ensure global inclusivity, the survey was localized into nine languages, including Portuguese, Italian, Chinese, and Spanish.

Data Integrity Note

Leadership explicitly acknowledges that historical data has been hampered by "duplicate" employee IDs, noted internally as a need to move past "shitty data." To ensure accurate routing and reporting, we implemented a "Location Dropdown" feature. This allows the CultureMonkey platform to merge location data with employee IDs, ensuring sentiment is accurately attributed to the correct departmental dashboards.

The strategic priority must be the conversion of Passives into Promoters to insulate the brand against talent poaching.

3. The RESPECT™ Model: Driver Analysis & Qualitative Sentiment

The engine of the Robertshaw experience is the correlation between respect and productivity. Where employees feel the RESPECT™ drivers are present, they "bloom" and "shine," providing a cultural anchor for the rest of the organization.

Top Performing Drivers

Analysis of the 17 underlying engagement drivers shows where the organization is most resilient:

* Alignment & Clarity: Employees report high clarity regarding company goals. Specifically, there is strong understanding of the three key results for People, Customer and Performance.
* Peer Commitment: A pervasive sense that fellow employees are committed to quality work.
* Safety & Environment: The "Work Environment" driver remains a major strength, with safety consistently prioritized—a critical cultural anchor for our manufacturing sites.

Critical Friction Points

Strategic attention is required where RESPECT™ drivers are failing to gain traction:

* Compensation & Market Alignment (Recognition): There is significant friction regarding the fairness of pay and benefits relative to the external market.
* Two-Way Communication (Supportive Feedback): A perceived lack of honest, open dialogue between management and staff remains a barrier to trust.
* Career Pathways (Empowerment): Tenured employees perceive a stagnation in career opportunity, which correlates with the drop in engagement scores over time.

Open-Text Sentiment Analysis

From the 9,532 comments received, the prevailing sentiment is one of "excitement" regarding the digital UI transition. However, qualitative data warns that simply "seeing the score" is no longer enough; the workforce expects the 2025 roadmap to reflect the feedback provided in these open-text fields.

6. Strategic Conclusions and Actionable Roadmap

The 2025 data mandates a shift from survey participation to cultural transformation. Our strategy will rest on three pillars:

* Pillar 1: Data Hygiene & Infrastructure We must resolve the "shitty data" issue. Cleaning historical records and resolving duplicate employee IDs is a prerequisite for a frictionless 2026 cycle. Data integrity is the first step toward building trust.
* Pillar 2: Operational Engagement We will launch a targeted intervention for the "Operations" segment and "Celaya P." This will involve using the high "Safety" scores as a bridge to improve "Two-Way Communication" and address the "Career Opportunity" deficit that plagues our tenured staff.
* Pillar 3: Enhancing the RESPECT Cycle We must move 249 managers from "score-watching" to "habit-forming." This requires a mandate for managers to utilize the "Action Recommendations" feature, specifically targeting the conversion of our 1,406 Passives into Promoters to drive the e-NPS into the "Good" bracket of 10–30.

Final Statement: As we execute this roadmap, we remain committed to the core principle of our culture: "In joy they bloom, like stars they shine, Happy employees spread a brand divine."
